Royal Achievements

The Longest-Reigning Monarch

On September 9, 2015, Queen Elizabeth II surpassed the record to become the longest-reigning monarch in British history, marking over 63 years on the throne. Her reign has witnessed remarkable changes, and her unwavering commitment has provided stability and continuity to the monarchy.

Strengthening the Commonwealth

Queen Elizabeth II has played a crucial role in fostering unity and cooperation within the Commonwealth. Her dedication to diplomatic relations and tireless efforts to promote shared values have contributed to maintaining strong ties among member nations. Her commitment to the Commonwealth reflects a deep sense of international responsibility.

Modernization of the Monarchy

Embracing modernity while preserving the traditions of the monarchy, Queen Elizabeth II has successfully adapted to societal changes. Her engagement with the public through various technological advancements, including television broadcasts, social media, and virtual engagements, reflects a forward-thinking approach, ensuring the monarchy remains relevant in the contemporary world.

She Took a Historic Visit to Ireland

Regarded as a historic trip to Ireland, the queen was the first British monarch to visit in 100 years. She was widely applauded for the four-day trip that took place in 2011. The visit demonstrated peace and an improved relationship between the two countries. President of Ireland Michael D. Higgins returned the gesture by vising the U.K. in 2014.

She Touched the Hearts of Many with Her Speeches

On December 25th, 2020, the Queen gave one of the most powerful speeches of the year. Her Christmas broadcast touched the hearts of many separated from family and friends due to the pandemic. “Of course, for many, this time of year will be tinged with sadness: some mourning the loss of those dear to them, and others missing friends and family members distanced for safety, when all they’d really want for Christmas is a simple hug or a squeeze of the hand,” the queen said in a video. “If you are among them, you are not alone, and let me assure you of my thoughts and prayers.”

She was a Pivotal Figure in International Relations

Traveling the world several times, the queen met with numerous notable figures. She met with every president of the United States during her reign, except Lyndon B. Johnson, reports NBCNews. She began meeting presidents at just age 25, when she was still a princess.

Queen Elizabeth II Supported Hundreds Of Charities

With her well-known love for animals, the Queen took the time to support hundreds of organizations ranging from wildlife conservation to education and arts. She has done more for charity than any other monarch in history, according to theguardian.com. Queen Elizabeth II has supported hundreds of British charities to raise £1.4bn. In Britain, the monarch supports a total of 2, 415 charities in Britain and almost 3,000 worldwide.
